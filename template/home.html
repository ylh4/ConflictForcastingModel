Conflict Forecasting Model (CFM)

Predicted vs Actual onset of conflict in Ethiopia (2019)

Overview

Armed conflict is pervasive in East African countries such as South Sudan and Ethiopia due to a range of reasons. Conflict also among groups with different ethnicity, religion, region, or livelihood backgrounds which is typically heightened by-elections, the implementation of controversial land tenure policies, federalism, state use of force, and the unintended effects of public and private investments in the horn of Africa. As a result, people are killed and displacement happens in mass due to conflict. Conflict also has a direct economic consewuences such us destruction of infrastructure, uncertainity indoing business, and increased military spending (ADB, 2018).

Appropriate early-warning systems that can meaningfully predict the onset of conflict is important to respond to crisis ahead of time. This will have a significant impact on reducing the devastating consequence of conflict in terms of loss of lives, livelihoods, and infrastructure.

The goal of this project is to allow users to forecast the onset of conflict in Ethiopia at subnational level and on monthly basis one year ahead of time.

Outputs

Our modeling work involves the use of conflict as an outcome variable extracted from the ACLED dataset. The ACLED project tracks armed conflict events on a real-time basis. Our model forecasts conflict in Ethiopia at the admin2/zoneal level as well as one year ahead of time and on a monthly basis.

To this end, the onset of conflict (cc_onset) is defined as a binary output variable representing 1, the presence of conflict; 0, otherwise.

Inputs

Below, we describe the list of input variables used in the model and their brief description.

Political and military

  • cc_onset(t-i): Previous onset of conflict
  • fatalities(t-i): Number of fatalities due to conflict
  • sd(t-i): Strategic development such as arrest of prominent persons or elections
  • cc_frequency(t-i): Frequency of conflict (months)
  • actors(t-i): Name of actors invloved in the conflict
  • event_type(t-i): type of event such as protest, riots, and use of force
  • TOT_POP(t-i): Number of population
  • TOT_LIT(2007): percent of litrate population
  • AGE_20-29 Percentage:(2007): percentage of youth population
  • et_d(2007): ethnic diversity index
  • religion_d(2007): religion diversity index
  • mobile_percentage(2007): mobile phone penetration rate
  • Television_percentage(2007): television penetration rate
  • Radio_percentage(2007): radio penetration rate
  • lc_d(t-i): land cover diversification index
  • mean_rf(t-i): monthly mean rainfall (mm)
